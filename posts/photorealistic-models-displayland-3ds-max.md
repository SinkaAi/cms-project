## Introduction

One of the main things to have realistic final renders is realistic models. Saying this is easier then actually doing it though. Creating 3D models can be a tedious work, that requires time and expertise. And usually this means that you need to either approach it with traditional modeling, or photogrammetry.

Both of these approaches have their Pros and Cons:
- **Traditional modeling** is good but you can't really get everything to be 100% realistic
- **Photogrammetry** will get you a 100% true representation but requires a lot of extra gear like cameras and software

Well today's post covers a third option that will try and get the best of both worlds.

## What is DisplayLand?

This is done with a product from the guys over at **Ubiquity6**. What I'm talking about is an APP for your phone called **DisplayLand**. The way that this APP works is almost like magic — technical magic so to speak.

Basically once you get the APP for your phone (Android and Apple) you basically have a scanner in your hand. All you have to do is choose an object that you would like to scan, take a short video of it, and that's all.

The main difference with regular photogrammetry is that you don't need to spend time taking 100+ images. The way it works is by taking a short 20-30 second video of the item you want to scan.

## Tips and Tricks

As a closing statement I would like to add that I actually had fun creating this video. And I can tell you from personal experience that doing it requires minimal effort and the results are great.

**Some ideas for using DisplayLand:**
- Next time you're on location at a client, grab your phone and take a few scans of certain objects they may have — then surprise them in the end!
- Take a few scans while on a hike, and then use those models for your scenes as fill assets

Get the APP and have some fun with it!
