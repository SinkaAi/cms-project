## Introduction

Today's tutorial covers a pretty interesting topic: **texturing**. I got an email from one of the subscribers asking how to texture an asset by hand in Vray.

Back in the day, this would have been done procedurally in 3ds Max. But since now we have access to more texture-specific software, why not use it?

Having said that, today's videos will cover **Substance Painter** and how to create multiple surfaces into one material. For ease of following, I've split this into two parts covering different topics.

## Creating the Base Material

Just to make it easier to follow, I chose to use a model of the most common teapot. All I did was combine it together, do a quick unwrap, and export it out.

**Important note:** Having a proper UVW unwrap is a MUST if you are going to use Substance Painter.

In the first video we will focus on how to create the **base layer** of the pot. This will allow us to see:
- How to control information from different layers
- How to limit certain information to particular layers

These are things you want to know before you start on anything more complex.

## Key Takeaways

- Substance Painter is a powerful alternative to procedural texturing in 3ds Max
- Multiple materials can be combined into one unified texture workflow
- Proper UVW unwrapping is essential for Substance Painter workflows
- The combination of Substance Painter + V-Ray produces excellent photorealistic results
